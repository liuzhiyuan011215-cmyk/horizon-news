## [Sally McKee, who coined 'memory wall,' has died](https://www.online-tribute.com/SallyMcKee) ⭐️ 6.0/10

Computer scientist Sally McKee, who introduced the influential term 'the memory wall,' has passed away. Her work highlighted the critical performance gap between processors and memory, a fundamental challenge that continues to shape computer architecture research and system design. McKee, along with her co-authors, formally described the 'memory wall' problem in a 1995 paper, arguing that memory access speed, not processor speed, would become the primary bottleneck for system performance.

rss · Hacker News · May 1, 14:45

**Background**: The 'memory wall' concept describes the growing disparity where processor speeds have historically increased much faster than memory access speeds. This gap creates a bottleneck, as the CPU often has to wait for data from memory, limiting overall system performance. The term has been central to discussions in computer architecture for decades.
